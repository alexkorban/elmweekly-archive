Issue #20

A round up of what’s happening in the wonderful world of the Elm programming language.

News

Elm 0.17.1

Evan Czaplicki released a new patch version of Elm this week: “everything is the same, just slightly better”.

Reading

Candy and Allowances: Parent-Child Communication in Elm

Brian Hicks gives us a thorough examination of parent-child component communication in Elm. 

Candy and Allowances, Part II: Child-Child Communication in Elm

Brian Hicks follows up with a discussion of child-child communication in this outstanding component communication series.

The Translator Pattern: a model for Child-to-Parent Communication in Elm

Continuing on the communication theme, Alex Lew coins the translator pattern.

Writing A Full Site in Phoenix and Elm

Brandon Richey makes HTTP calls from Elm in part 2 of his Phoenix and Elm series.

Elm & the DOM

Søren Debois looks at how to get dimensions from the DOM in Elm.

A beginner’s guide to JSON and Elm

Mario Martinez walks you through JSON Decoding in this tutorial.

Elmtastic Updates

Get your head around the updates in Elm 0.17 with help from Dave Thomas.

Watching & Listening

Elmseeds

Short screencasts to teach you the Elm programming language from Erik Person. Each video tutorial walks through a library, tool, or language feature.

Testing en Elm

Get an overview of testing in Elm 0.17 from Thomas Brisbout’s lightning talk at the Elm Paris meetup. (In French)

Code

elm-mdl-demo

Elm MDL, Google’s Material Design Lite for Elm, gets a new release at version 6.0.0 (announcement).

Elm-UI Kitchen Sink

Elm-UI, a GUI library and framework for Elm, gets an update for 0.17

Elm Polymer

Thoughts on how to integrate Polymer with Elm with copious code examples by Kevin Le Brun.

Pocket Triage

Kevin Le Brun has also built a small SPA in Elm offering a simple way to clean up your Pocket links in next to no time.

Porting to Elm the Maori dictionary project (originally in Flex)

elm-maori-dictionary is a port of the Maori Dictionary project to Elm by Marielle Lange.

Plogger: Piping Logger

Marcel van Pinxteren releases a piping logger inspired by Elm, and of interest to anyone doing Elixir.
