Issue #35

A round up of what’s happening in the wonderful world of the Elm programming language.

Reading

Building A React/Redux/Elm Bridge

A. Sharif provides a detailed look at integrating Elm into a JavaScript application with a focus on React and Redux.

Copy to System Clipboard in Elm

Clayton Flesher looks at the seamless of integration of Clipboard.js with Elm.

Elm: Commands and Subscriptions and Ports. Oh My!

Bill St. Clair explains some of the more challenging concepts he encountered when learning Elm.

JSON Schema Changes: A Halloween Horror Story

Brian Hicks looks at dealing with changes in your JSON schema.

Managed effects and Elm

Kevin Welcher provides an analogy for thinking about effects in Elm.

Elm Maybe – Handling problems explicitly

Dennis Reimann reprises his Elm series from earlier in the year by looking at Maybe.

Zach Kessin Elm Testing Book (a book on using elm-test)
Kyle J. Kress Crocodiles and Alligators (using Lenses elsewhere)
Jack Franklin Building the Elm Playground (a new Elm blog)
Elm Lang on the App Store (iOS app with learning resources)

Watching & Listening

ElmLive: Codevember Day 1

Aaron VonderHaar live streams a coding session drawing random walks on a grid.

Elm Tutorial Part 6: Calculator

Tensor rounds off his Elm screencast series by building a calculator, looking at refactoring and modules.

Why Elm is Different

Daniel Walker’s talk at Fort Collins Internet Professionals (code).

The Return of Stream I/O

André Medeiros mentions Elm in his look at the breakthrough of Stream I/O into frontend development.

Code

Plotting in Elm!

Tereza Sokol has made a plotting library in Elm.

keyboard-combo 2.0.0

Handle keyboard key combinations with type safety in Elm by Scott Corgan.

Pythagoras Tree

A cool interactive Pythagoras tree visualisation by Samuel Mueller (code).

IV Drip Worksheet

A cool visualisation of administering a medicinal drip to animals by Brian Marick.

Elm Round

Round floats (mathematically/commercially) to a given number of decimal places by Matthias.

Todo App in Haskell and Elm

Todo app built with Haskell (server-side) and Elm (client-side) by Jens Krause.

Elm Greeting

Chris Buttery says hello and goodbye in Elm.

Bad Mamma Jamma

Andrew Hoglund built a collaborative music app in Elixir and Elm to show off at Empire City Elixir.

elm-quickstart

Elm, SASS, Gulp, Bootstrap and JS synchronisation by Pietro Grandi.
